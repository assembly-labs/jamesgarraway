üìÖ 4-Week Practice Schedule

A structured plan to master fractions in one month

How to Use This Schedule

This 4-week schedule combines lessons, practice, and review to help you master 3rd grade fractions. Each session is designed to take 20-30 minutes.

üìå Important Tips:
  • Practice at the same time each day for best results
  • Take breaks if you feel frustrated - it's part of learning!
  • Complete the diagnostic first to identify your starting point
  • Rest days are important - your brain needs time to process!
1

Week 1: Skills Check & Equal Parts

Focus: Understanding what makes parts equal

Monday 20-30 min
  • Take Skills Check (Optional)
  • Review results with parent/teacher
  • Set goals for the month
Tuesday 25 min
  • Lesson 1: Equal Parts
  • Complete hands-on activities
  • Start worksheet (page 1)
Wednesday 20 min
  • Finish Lesson 1 worksheet
  • Flashcards: Concepts (5 min)
  • Review with answer key
Thursday 20 min
  • Mixed Review: Focus on equal parts
  • Number Line Tool: Halves
Friday 15 min
  • Review Lesson 1 concepts
  • Flashcards practice
  • Play fraction games
Weekend
Rest & Review

Optional: Share what you learned with family!

üí° Week 1 Tips
The diagnostic helps identify what you already know - don't stress!
Use real objects (pizza, paper, toys) to practice equal parts
Draw pictures to show your thinking

Focus: Fractions bigger than 1

Monday 25 min
  • Lesson 2: Improper Fractions
  • Watch demonstration
  • Try examples together
Tuesday 20 min
  • Complete Lesson 2 worksheet
  • Practice converting fractions
  • Self-check answers
Wednesday 20 min
  • Mixed Review
  • Number Line: Mixed numbers
Thursday 15 min
  • Flashcards: All categories
  • Review Lessons 1-2
  • Practice with parent

  • Challenge: Mix review quiz

Optional: Find fractions in sports scores!

üí° Week 2 Tips
Think of improper fractions as "more than one whole"
Use football quarters: 5/4 = 1 game + 1 quarter
Draw circles to represent wholes and parts

  • Lesson 4: Subtracting Fractions

Only add/subtract numerators - keep denominator the same!

üí° Week 4 Tips
Simplifying means finding the smallest way to say the same fraction
Equivalent fractions are like different names for the same number
If you struggle with any concept, revisit that lesson!

After Week 4: Keep Practicing!

Congratulations on completing the 4-week program! Here are ways to maintain your skills:

  • Daily Practice: Spend 10 minutes on flashcards or mixed review
  • Real-World Fractions: Look for fractions in cooking, sports, and time
  • Help Others: Teaching someone else reinforces your understanding
  • Challenge Yourself: Try harder problems or time yourself
  • Stay Consistent: Regular short practice beats occasional long sessions
