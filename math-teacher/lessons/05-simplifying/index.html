Coach's Lesson: Simplifying Fractions

The WHY: Would you rather say "I ate 4 out of 8 slices" or "I ate half the pizza"? They mean the SAME thing, but one is easier to understand! Simplifying makes fractions easier to work with and compare.

The Rule: 2/4 and 1/2 are the same amount! Simplifying means finding the simplest way to write a fraction by dividing both numbers by their greatest common factor (GCF).

How to Simplify: Find a number that divides BOTH the top AND bottom evenly. Divide both by that number. Keep going until you can't divide anymore. That's simplest form!

Football Connection: Caleb Williams of the Bears completed 12 of 36 passes against a tough defense. That's 12/36. Both divide by 12! So 12√∑12 = 1, and 36√∑12 = 3. His completion rate is 1/3. When DJ Moore asks about the stats, coach Ben Johnson says "1/3" - same stat, much easier to say!
