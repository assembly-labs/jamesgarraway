Part E: Explain Your Thinking

12 Justin Jefferson says: "When I add 1/4 + 2/4, I get 3/8 because 1+2=3 and 4+4=8." Is Justin correct? Why or why not?

üìù Answer Key

1. 2/5 + 1/5 = 3/5 (Drawing should show 3 out of 5 parts shaded)

2. 3/8 + 2/8 = 5/8 (Drawing should show 5 out of 8 slices shaded)

3. 4/6 + 1/6 = 5/6 (Drawing should show 5 out of 6 parts shaded)

4. 1/4 + 2/4 = 3/4

5. 3/10 + 4/10 = 7/10

6. 2/8 + 5/8 = 7/8

7. 1/6 + 4/6 = 5/6

8. 3/10 + 2/10 = 5/10 of the field = 50 yards

9. 4/10 + 5/10 = 9/10. Not more than one whole field.

10. 3/4 + 2/4 = 5/4 = 1 1/4

11. 5/6 + 4/6 = 9/6 = 1 3/6 (or 1 1/2 if simplified)

12. No, Justin is not correct. When we add fractions with the same denominator, we add the numerators but keep the denominator the same. The pieces are still fourths (the size doesn't change), so we have 3 fourths, not 3 eighths. The correct answer is 3/4. We don't add denominators because we're counting pieces of the same size - they stay fourths!
